Beautiful code is a liability

January 2026

The developers who survive 2026 won't be the best coders. They'll be the fastest.

The best coders I know are panicking. Not publicly. In Slack DMs. In nervous jokes about AI taking their jobs. In long threads defending practices that won't matter in eighteen months.

They were never product people. Their product was the code. And it just got commoditized by AI.

So while they're arguing about how AI doesn't write elegant code, they ignore it writes working code. In seconds. While they're still debating architecture patterns.

Average code shipped fast beats perfect code shipped next month. It always did. The difference now is that "today" means minutes, not months.

Engineers advocating for clean abstractions and proper patterns aren't wrong about craft. They're wrong about what matters. They're protecting a status quo that valued their expertise, but that expertise is devaluing fast.

Then there's the other crowd. The ones who use AI but spend hours "fixing" its output. Refactoring generated code to meet their standards. Adding abstractions. Making it pretty.

They missed the point entirely.

Code has become disposable. You'll regenerate it next week when requirements change. That refactor you're so proud of? It becomes debt the moment you commit it. You're polishing something that was never meant to last.

Your energy should be on deciding what to build. Understand what users actually need. Know when to cut scope. Ship something embarrassing and learn from it.

You're not competing with AI. You're competing with developers who use AI and don't flinch.

They're not waiting for your code review. Or their own.
